Headings

Headings in HTML are similar to headings in other types of media. For example, in newspapers, large headings are typically used to capture a reader’s attention. Other times, headings are used to describe content, like the title of a movie or an educational article.

HTML follows a similar pattern. In HTML, there are six different headings, or heading elements. Preview: Docs There are 6 heading elements in HTML. They are labeled 1-6, with 1 being the largest sized heading and 6 being the smallest. These are great for labeling different sections in a website. Headings can be used for a variety of purposes, like titling sections, articles, or other forms of content.

    The following is the list of heading elements available in HTML. They are ordered from largest to smallest in size.

  1. <h1> — used for main headings. All other smaller headings are used for subheadings.
  2. <h2> This is the second-level heading, used right after <h1>. It’s for main section titles on a webpage.
  3. <h3> Used for sub-sections under an <h2> heading. It’s slightly smaller and shows a lower level of importance.
  4. <h4> Represents a sub-section within an <h3> heading. Used when you need more detailed structure.
  5. <h5> Shows even less importance, used for deep sub-points. Rarely used unless your content has many levels.
  6. <h6> The smallest and least important heading. Used when you need very fine structure or detail.
